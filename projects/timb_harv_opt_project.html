GIS for Timber Harvest Optimization in Washington State

This project aimed to tackle the environmental and logistical contrains behind timber sourcing for construction resource planning and project management.

My research sought to identify the most optimal areas for timber harvesting after excluding intersections with Washington's DNR Habitat Conservation Lands and abandoned forest roads.The analysis focused on three key datasets: active timber harvest applications, DNR Habitat Conservation Lands, and orphaned or abandoned forest roads.

  • To create the map, I first filtered the timber harvest dataset to include only active applications expiring after January 1, 2026.
  • I then used the ‘Check Validity’ tool to separate and retain valid geometries.
  • A 1-mile buffer was applied around abandoned roads, and overlapping harvest areas were removed using the ‘Difference’ geoprocessing tool.
  • The same process was repeated to exclude areas intersecting with conservation lands.

The final map displays timber harvest zones that are active, long-term, and located entirely outside buffered abandoned roads and conservation lands.
