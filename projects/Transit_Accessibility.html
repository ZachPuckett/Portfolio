Mind the Gap: Assessing Transit Equity in Seattle

This project investigates the geography of public transit accessibility across Seattle, with a focus on equity outcomes. Using ArcGIS and demographic data, I analyzed which neighborhoods fall outside a 10-minute walking radius of transit stops, particularly in relation to households in poverty and without vehicles.

  • Used ArcGIS Pro to perform spatial joins and proximity analysis on Seattle's GTFS transit stop data and census block groups.
  • Mapped households more than a 10-minute walk from a transit stop, identifying underserved neighborhoods through a binary accessibility index.
  • Incorporated ACS 2023 data on income, vehicle ownership, and total population to highlight areas with compounded transportation disadvantage.
  • Produced thematic maps showing accessibility overlaps with low-income and vehicle-less households, isolating South Seattle as a critical intervention zone.

The findings showed that while neighborhoods like North Beach and Madison Park are also underserved by transit, only South Seattle demonstrates high transit inaccessibility combined with economic and mobility vulnerabilities. This project supports the need for targeted policy that prioritizes transit equity based on socioeconomic risk.
