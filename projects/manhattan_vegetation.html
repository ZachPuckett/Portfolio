Visualizing Vegetation Change in Manhattan with Google Earth Engine

Project Overview

This animated gif showcases the capabilities of Google Earth Engine to use satellite imagery for remote sensing.

The project focused on detecting vegetation change in Manhattan by compositing and comparing satellite imagery throughout 2022.

Images were filtered by date and cloud cover from the Sentinel-2 satellite collection, clipped to Manhattan’s boundary, and processed into monthly median composites for the year 2022. The Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) was calculated for each month to visualize seasonal vegetation patterns across Manhattan.

  • Sentinel-2 imagery was filtered by cloud cover, geographic bounds, and acquisition date for 2022.
  • Each month’s imagery was turned into a median composite to reduce cloud artifacts and noise.
  • NDVI was calculated by comparing near-infrared and red bands to measure vegetation presence.
