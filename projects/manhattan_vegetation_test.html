Visualizing Vegetation Change in Manhattan with Google Earth Engine

Project Overview

This animated gif showcases my ability to use Google Earth Engine for remote sensing. The project focuses on detecting vegetation change in Manhattan by compositing and comparing satellite imagery throughout 2024.

Methodology

Images were filtered by date and cloud cover from the Sentinel-2 satellite collection, clipped to Manhattan’s boundary, and processed into monthly median composites for the year 2024.

The Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) was calculated for each month to visualize seasonal vegetation patterns across Manhattan.

NDVI = (NIR - Red) / (NIR + Red)

  • Sentinel-2 imagery was filtered by cloud cover, geographic bounds, and acquisition date for 2024.
  • Each month’s imagery was turned into a median composite to reduce cloud artifacts and noise.
  • NDVI was calculated by comparing near-infrared and red bands to measure vegetation presence.
