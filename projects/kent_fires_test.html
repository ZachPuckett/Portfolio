ArcGIS for Mapping 2016 Kent Fire Department Incidents

This project analyzed emergency service calls in the Kent Fire Department coverage area using ArcGIS Spatial Analyst to identify incident risk zones and inform better resource deployment.

The dataset included synthetic subsets of 2016 emergency calls representing a variety of occupancies, including residential, commercial, industrial, and warehousing zones. The analysis focused on density and distribution patterns across five types of emergency responses.

  • The Kernel Density geoprocessing tool was used to model the spatial distribution of emergency incidents for each category.
  • Each incident category (e.g., Structure Fires, EMS, Service Calls, HazMat) was mapped as a separate density raster layer for precise spatial analysis.
  • Custom contour polylines were generated for each map to illustrate variations in density and to visually represent zones of heightened risk.
  • All maps were produced within ArcGIS and used consistent styling and extent for comparison across response types.

Key Findings

  • Structure Fires were concentrated heavily in West and Southwest Kent, indicating a persistent risk zone for structural emergencies.
  • Rescue/EMS incidents were more widely distributed across the city, but still centered heavily in West and Central Kent.
  • Service Calls and “Other” incidents followed a similar distribution to EMS, showing notable clustering in Central Kent alongside the dominant West Kent region.
  • Fire, Explosion, and HazMat calls also peaked in West and Central Kent, but diminished rapidly outside those areas.
  • The combined map of all emergency responses revealed a hotspot at the intersection of West and Central Kent, underscoring the area's elevated emergency activity overall.
