Mapping SNAP Benefit Use Across Washington State

This project visualizes the geographic distribution of Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) usage across Washington at both the county and census tract levels.

The aim is to identify areas of high SNAP enrollment and guide equitable allocation of funding and outreach resources, especially in underserved communities.

Methodology

  • SNAP usage data was provided as household-level counts in two shapefiles — one for census tracts and one for counties — and joined directly to corresponding polygon layers in QGIS.
  • For both scales, I normalized SNAP households by total households to compute percentage-based usage rates.
  • New fields were created using the QGIS Field Calculator, and classification schemes (quantiles and natural breaks) were tested for visual clarity and regional comparison.
  • Choropleth symbology was applied using color ramps, and map elements were composed in the Print Layout tool with consistent legends, labels, and titles.

Data Sources: American Community Survey 5-Year Estimates, U.S. Census Bureau; Washington State Office of Financial Management shapefiles (county and tract boundaries with SNAP-related household data).
