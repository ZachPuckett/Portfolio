GIS for Mapping Seattle

This project developed a Health Equity Index for Seattle census tracts by combining health-related indicators to reveal spatial patterns of disadvantage using ArcGIS Pro.

The analysis incorporated five variables—including insurance coverage and chronic illness rates—selected from public datasets, and standardized them into z-scores to form a composite index indicating areas of poor health outcomes.

  • Health variables were selected based on variation, breadth, and non-redundancy using correlation plots.
  • Each selected variable was standardized to z-scores, then averaged (with or without weighting) to create the Health Equity Index.
  • Quintile classifications were used to identify areas of very low to very high equity needs, visualized with choropleth maps.
  • ArcGIS Pro tools including ‘Standardize Field’ and ‘Calculate Composite Index’ were used throughout, supporting both manual and automated workflows.

Key Findings

  • Structure Fires were concentrated heavily in West and Southwest Kent, indicating a persistent risk zone for structural emergencies.
  • Rescue/EMS incidents were more widely distributed across the city, but still centered heavily in West and Central Kent.
  • Service Calls and “Other” incidents followed a similar distribution to EMS, showing notable clustering in Central Kent alongside the dominant West Kent region.
  • Fire, Explosion, and HazMat calls also peaked in West and Central Kent, but diminished rapidly outside those areas.
  • The combined map of all emergency responses revealed a hotspot at the intersection of West and Central Kent, underscoring the area's elevated emergency activity overall.
